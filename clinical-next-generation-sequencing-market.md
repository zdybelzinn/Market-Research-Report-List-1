<p><p>The Clinical Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) market includes various types such as targeted sequencing, exome sequencing, and others. Targeted sequencing focuses on specific genes or regions of interest, allowing precise identification of mutations related to particular diseases. Exome sequencing analyzes the exons, or coding regions, of genes, providing insights into genetic disorders. Other NGS approaches encompass whole genome sequencing and RNA sequencing, offering broader data sets for comprehensive insights into genetic variations and their implications in clinical settings.</p></p>

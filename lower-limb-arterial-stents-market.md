<p><p>Lower limb arterial stents are medical devices implanted in the arteries of the legs to treat peripheral artery disease (PAD), a condition characterized by narrowed arteries that decrease blood flow to the limbs. These stents help restore proper blood flow, alleviate symptoms, and reduce the risk of severe complications. The market for lower limb arterial stents is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, a rising aging population, and advancements in stent technology. </p><p>Market growth is further enhanced by the growing awareness of PAD and the benefits of minimally invasive procedures. Increased healthcare expenditure and supportive reimbursement policies are also contributing to market expansion. <p><p>The lower limb arterial stents market consists of several types tailored for vascular procedures. Balloon-expandable stents are temporarily compressed and expanded using a balloon catheter, providing precise placement. Self-expandable stents utilize a unique material that allows for automatic expansion upon deployment, adapting to the artery's shape. Bare metal stents offer structural support without any coating, promoting immediate blood flow. Other types might include drug-eluting stents or bioresorbable stents that aim to minimize restenosis and improve long-term outcomes.</p></p>

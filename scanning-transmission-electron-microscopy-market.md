<p><p>The Scanning Transmission Electron Microscopy (STEM) market encompasses several analytical techniques, primarily focusing on Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy (EELS) and Energy-Dispersive X-Ray Spectroscopy (EDX or EDS). EELS allows researchers to measure energy losses of electrons interacting with materials, providing insights into electronic structures and chemical compositions. EDX, on the other hand, detects emitted X-rays to analyze elemental composition. Additional techniques classified as "Others" may include various imaging and spectroscopic methods that enhance material characterization capabilities in STEM applications.</p></p>

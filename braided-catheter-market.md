<p><p>The braided catheter market is categorized into three main types based on material composition: metals, polymers, and other materials. Metal braided catheters, often made of stainless steel or nitinol, offer enhanced strength and kink resistance, ideal for challenging procedures. Polymer catheters, commonly constructed from materials like polyurethane or silicone, provide flexibility and biocompatibility, suitable for various medical applications. Other materials may include composites or specialized blends, which combine properties of both metals and polymers to meet specific performance requirements in diverse clinical settings.</p></p>

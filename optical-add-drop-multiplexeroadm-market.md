<p><p>An Optical Add-Drop Multiplexer (OADM) is a device used in fiber optic networks to add or drop specific wavelengths of light without disrupting other signals. The market features two primary types: Fixed OADMs, which are designed for specific wavelengths and provide a simple solution for dedicated routing, and Reconfigurable OADMs, which allow for dynamic management of wavelengths, enabling operators to adapt to changing network demands and optimize capacity use. Both types enhance the efficiency and flexibility of optical communications.</p></p>
